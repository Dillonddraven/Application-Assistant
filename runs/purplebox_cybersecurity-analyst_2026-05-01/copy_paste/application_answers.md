# Application Answers

### How has your education and project work prepared you for this entry-level role?
My coursework targeted what entry-level analysts do day to day. In an incident response course I built a playbook that started from a business impact analysis, identified sales as the essential function, and sequenced restoration so sales-supporting systems came back online first. I also designed a tiered cloud backup schedule that placed frequently accessed data in fast-retrieval storage and infrequently accessed data in lower-cost archival tiers, balancing recovery time and cost. Alongside that, I built SAST ingestion and vulnerability reporting during my Dillard's internship, completed a centralized logging lab with alerts on authentication activity, used Wireshark for traffic analysis, and produced CMMC v2 and NIST SP 800-171 documentation. I am ready to monitor events, triage findings, and escalate when needed.

### Describe your experience with vulnerability management.
I turned vulnerability data into remediation guidance by building a SAST reporting workflow that ingested findings from GitLab and produced clean Excel reports with severity breakdowns, team and repository views, and visual summaries. The value was making findings understandable. Stakeholders gained a clear view across repos and could prioritize remediation based on severity and ownership instead of parsing raw scanner output.

### Describe your experience with security logs and monitoring.
I configured log forwarding from Linux and Windows into a centralized environment, validated event ingestion, reviewed authentication activity, and troubleshot cases where expected events did not appear. I also built a Python script to classify log entries into clearer categories, which improved visibility into system activity and made it easier to spot security-relevant patterns. That work covered generating events, forwarding logs, validating collection, classifying activity, and using the results to support investigation.
